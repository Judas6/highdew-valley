# Highdew Valley - Game Design Document

**Game Title:** Highdew Valley  
**Genre:** Farming RPG / Life Sim (Stardew Valley inspired)  
**Theme:** Cannabis Cultivation in a Magical, Wholesome Rural Valley  
**Platform Target:** Mobile (Android/iOS) via Godot or Unity export; Prototype in Pygame + HTML5 demo  
**Tone:** Cozy, positive, creative, community-focused, respectful of cannabis culture (medicinal, recreational, artistic, sustainable). No glorification of abuse; focus on craftsmanship, connection, nature.  
**Art Style:** Pixel art, vibrant greens, earth tones, warm lighting. 16-bit inspired like Stardew Valley.  
**Music:** Chill lo-fi beats with acoustic guitar, nature sounds, occasional upbeat festival tracks. Infused with subtle "haze" ambient.  

## Core Premise
You inherit your late Uncle Bud's old farm in the secluded Highdew Valley. The valley's unique microclimate and "spirit soil" make it perfect for growing the world's most prized cannabis strains. Restore the farm, master cultivation, befriend the tight-knit community of growers, artisans, and dreamers, explore ancient mines for rare minerals, fish pristine rivers, and become a legend in the cannabis world. Build relationships, participate in strain festivals, upgrade your homestead, and uncover the valley's gentle mysteries.

The entire economy, culture, and daily life revolves around premium, sustainable cannabis cultivation ("green gold"). All Stardew Valley features are rethemed:
- Crops → Cannabis strains (different effects: relaxing Indica, uplifting Sativa, balanced Hybrids)
- Animals → Pollinator hives, beneficial insects, "mother plants" for cloning, perhaps themed "Kush goats" or "Hemp hens" for byproducts (fiber, compost)
- Tools → Hoe, Watering Can, Scythe/Trimmer, Nutrient Sprayer, Curing Rack
- Mining → Crystal Caverns for terpene-rich minerals and resin geodes used in super-fertilizers or concentrates
- Fishing → River with "legendary" fish tied to old grow legends
- Social → Heart events involving grow help, strain sharing, creative collaborations, "session" talks
- Festivals → Themed around seasons and strain types (e.g., Harvest Haze Festival with judging)
- Processing → Drying, curing, making edibles, oils, topicals, rosin
- Magic → "Alchemical" fertilizers, moon-phase growing, spirit plant lore

## World & Town: Highdew Valley / Highgrove

**Layout (Top-down view similar to Stardew):**
- **Your Farm (South of town):** Large plot with tillable soil, old barn (upgrade to processing shed, drying room, greenhouse), small pond or well. Expandable fields. Secret spot for "mother plants".
- **Town Square (Central):** Leaf fountain/statue ("The Eternal Bud" – artistic leaf sculpture), paths radiating out. Bulletin board for quests/special orders. 
- **The Emerald Emporium (General Store - Penny):** Sells tools, basic supplies, gifts, daily needs. Gossip central.
- **The Strain Sanctuary (Seed Shop/Nursery - Flora):** Premium and rare seeds, seedlings, basic nutrients, growing guides. Breeding station for custom crosses (endgame).
- **The Forge & Glass (Blacksmith - Hammer):** Tool upgrades, glass pipes/bongs (themed as decorative or functional "art tools"), metalwork for greenhouses.
- **The Haze Haven Lounge (Saloon - Jazz):** Chill spot. Buy infused teas, mocktails, light edibles. Play simple minigames (perhaps "strain matching" or music rhythm). Heart events here.
- **The Green Grove Clinic (Herbalist/Doctor - Dr. Sage):** "Healing" (restore energy/stamina), research quests for medicinal strains, buy topicals or balms that give temporary buffs.
- **The Mystic Spire (Alchemist - Zephyr):** Rare/seasonal seeds, advanced biodynamic fertilizers, "lunar elixirs", crystal-infused nutrients. Mysterious quests.
- **The Woodwright's Workshop (Carpenter - Willow):** House upgrades, barn/greenhouse construction/expansion, furniture (cozy cabin aesthetic with hemp accents).
- **River's Edge (Fishing - Finn):** Dock, fishing spots. Some areas only accessible after certain relationships or upgrades.
- **Crystal Caverns (Mines - Grit):** Multi-level mines. Combat light (pest creatures like "mite swarms" or "mold slimes"). Ores = minerals for fertilizer. Rare "resin crystals" and geodes.
- **Community Grove (Old Center):** Dilapidated building. Restore via "Grower's Guild Bundles" – donate specific high-quality strains, processed goods, minerals, artifacts. Rewards: Better community greenhouse access, town improvements.
- **Other:** Library/Museum (strain history, artifacts), Beach access if expanded, Festival grounds, NPC houses scattered (cozy cottages with grow plots).

**Seasons & Growing:**
- **Spring:** Focus on vegetative growth, balanced hybrids. Outdoor planting starts.
- **Summer:** Peak flower for many sativas. Heat management important.
- **Fall:** Indica harvest season. Curing weather good.
- **Winter:** Mostly indoor/greenhouse. Force flowering with lights. Forage winter herbs. Festivals indoors.
- **Greenhouses:** Essential for year-round and climate control. Upgrade for better yields, auto-water, etc.
- **Growth Stages:** Seedling (1-2 days) -> Vegetative (water + N nutrients) -> Pre-flower -> Flowering (P/K nutrients, light) -> Harvest ready. Quality affected by care, environment, genetics.
- **Pests & Care:** Water daily or use sprinklers. Nutrients, pH balance (themed simply). Treat powdery mildew, spider mites with organic sprays or beneficial bugs. Over/under water = lower quality.
- **Quality Tiers:** Common -> Good -> Excellent -> Legendary (perfect care + special conditions). Affects sell price, gift value, "effects" description when used/gifted.

## Mechanics (All Stardew Features Adapted)

**Farming/Cultivation Loop (Core Gameplay):**
1. Till soil (hoe).
2. Buy/plant seeds (different strains have different grow times, preferred seasons, base yields, effects).
3. Water (can or sprinklers).
4. Apply nutrients/fertilizer at right stages.
5. Monitor for pests (scout or auto).
6. Harvest at peak (trimmer/scythe for speed/quality).
7. Process: Hang to dry (time), cure in jars (better quality over time), or process into edibles/oils/rosin (crafting).
8. Sell at co-op/dispensary or fulfill special orders for bonus gold/reputation.
9. Propagate: Take clones from mother plants for consistent genetics (advanced).

**Example Strains (expandable list):**
- **Blue Dream (Hybrid, Spring/Summer):** Balanced, creative/relaxed. Good all-rounder.
- **Sour Diesel (Sativa, Spring):** Energizing, uplifting, focus. Fast veg.
- **OG Kush (Indica, Summer/Fall):** Deep relaxation, body high. High yield potential.
- **Girl Scout Cookies (Hybrid, Summer):** Sweet, euphoric, appetite. Popular.
- **Northern Lights (Indica, Fall):** Classic relaxing, easy grow, resilient.
- **Durban Poison (Sativa, Spring/Fall):** Pure sativa energy, landrace vibes.
- **Granddaddy Purple (Indica, Fall/Winter indoor):** Sweet grape, strong body, beautiful colors.
- **Rare/Legendary:** "Spirit Haze" (found in mines or special quests) – unique effects, high value.
- **Wild/Forage:** "Valley Hemp" – lower THC but hardy, good for fiber/compost or beginner.

**Crafting & Processing:**
- Drying racks, curing jars.
- Infused edibles (brownies, gummies – Sugar helps or you craft).
- Concentrates: Rosin press (late game), oils, tinctures.
- Topicals: Balms for "healing".
- Gifts: Pre-rolled "joints" (themed as nice packaging), strain-specific bouquets.

**Skills & Progression:**
- **Cultivation:** Better yields, quality, unlock advanced techniques (LST, topping, SCROG – simplified as "plant training" perks).
- **Foraging:** Find wild seeds, rare herbs for teas or recipes.
- **Fishing:** Better catches, legendary fish tied to lore.
- **Mining:** Deeper levels, better minerals, combat perks.
- **Crafting:** Better processing efficiency, new recipes.
- **Social:** More heart events, better gifts reception, marriage perks (spouse helps on farm).

**Social System:**
- 12+ main NPCs with full schedules, unique dialogues per season/day/heart level,  heart events (6-8 per romanceable).
- Gift system: Strains they like (based on personality/effect preference), crafted goods, flowers/herbs.
- Romanceable: Most adults. Marriage: Spouse moves in or you can live together, helps with chores, unique dialogues, possible "family" (adopted or "hemp baby" fun).
- Non-romance: Mayor, doctor, etc. Still deep friendships.

**Festivals (4 main + minor):**
- **Spring Seedling Celebration:** Planting contests, new seed releases.
- **Summer Solstice Uplift:** Sativa-focused, music, energy activities.
- **Fall Harvest Haze Festival:** Main event – submit best buds for judging (quality, yield, aroma, effects description). Big rewards, community party.
- **Winter Lights & Cures:** Indoor grow displays, curing contests, cozy gatherings, gift exchanges.
- Special orders and community quests year-round.

**Exploration & Other:**
- Mines with light combat and resource gathering.
- Fishing with rod upgrades.
- Foraging in woods/riverbanks for wild cannabis or companion plants.
- Artifact hunting for museum (old pipes, strain journals, ancient tools).
- House customization for cozy vibe.
- Endgame: Become master grower, perhaps open your own dispensary or strain breeding lab, perfect the ultimate strain, or protect the valley from external threats (light story).

## NPCs - Full Roster with Histories, Personalities, and Visual Descriptions

**1. Mayor Elias Greenleaf**  
**Role:** Mayor, quest giver for town projects.  
**Appearance (for image gen):** Middle-aged man, warm kind eyes, short salt-and-pepper beard, wearing a brown tweed vest over a green shirt, leaf-shaped pin on lapel, holding a simple wooden pipe (decorative). Friendly, approachable expression. Pixel art Stardew style, front portrait.  
**History:** Born and raised in Highdew Valley. His grandfather was instrumental in legalizing and organizing small-scale cultivation after the old prohibition days in the region's lore. Elias left briefly for university to study sustainable agriculture and community development, then returned when a large corporation attempted a hostile takeover of local farms. He rallied the growers, formed the co-op, and has led ever since with a vision of prosperity without exploitation. He sees you as a fresh hope for the valley's future.  
**Personality:** Diplomatic, optimistic, paternal. Loves hearing about your grows and community ideas.  
**Likes:** High-quality hybrids, community event contributions, sustainable practices.  
**Dislikes:** Corporate greed, environmental harm, laziness.  
**Schedule Example:** Mornings at Town Hall, afternoons walking fields or talking to residents, evenings at home or lounge.  
**Heart Events Ideas:** Help organize a festival, mediate a dispute between growers, share a special strain that reminds him of his grandfather.

**2. Flora "Bloom" Evergreen**  
**Role:** Owner of Strain Sanctuary (seeds, seedlings, advice).  
**Appearance:** Young woman in her late 20s, vibrant shoulder-length green-dyed hair with flower clips, dirt smudges on cheeks, wearing practical overalls over a white tank top, holding a potted seedling and clipboard. Enthusiastic smile, green thumb vibe. Pixel art front portrait.  
**History:** Orphaned as a child and taken in by valley elders. While exploring the old growth forests as a teen, she discovered a unique wild hybrid landrace that became the foundation for many of the valley's signature strains. Self-taught geneticist and passionate breeder. She runs the nursery to share knowledge and preserve biodiversity. She sees potential in every seed – and in you.  
**Personality:** Bubbly plant nerd, always excited about new crosses or growth milestones. Slightly clumsy in social but genuine.  
**Likes:** Rare or unusual seeds/strains, successful breeding results, detailed grow journals.  
**Dislikes:** Monocultures, GMOs (in this world's context), neglect of plants.  
**Heart Events:** Collaborate on breeding a new strain together, help rescue a failing crop of hers, romantic greenhouse date under grow lights.

**3. "Hammer" Stone (Reginald Stone)**  
**Role:** Blacksmith / Forge & Glass owner. Tool upgrades and artistic glass.  
**Appearance:** Burly, muscular man in his 40s, short buzz cut or bald with stubble, wearing a thick leather apron over a simple shirt, soot marks, big calloused hands holding a hammer or beautiful glass pipe. Gruff but warm smile. Pixel art.  
**History:** Worked construction in the city until a serious injury left him seeking a quieter life. Discovered the valley while recuperating and never left. The physical work of forging tools and the creative outlet of shaping glass (pipes, bongs, decorative pieces) helped his recovery. He now supplies the community with reliable tools and beautiful functional art. Has a soft spot for those who work with their hands.  
**Personality:** Gruff exterior, big heart. Speaks little but actions speak loud. Loyal once you earn trust.  
**Likes:** Well-crafted tools, strong indica for recovery, honest hard work.  
**Dislikes:** Shoddy craftsmanship, complaining without action.  
**Heart Events:** Forge a special custom tool together, help him with a big glass commission, deep talk about overcoming pain (physical/emotional).

**4. Jasper "Jazz" Haze**  
**Role:** Owner of The Haze Haven Lounge. Social hub.  
**Appearance:** Slim man in his 30s, long dreadlocks or relaxed hair, stylish sunglasses, colorful patterned shirt unbuttoned over tank, relaxed lean against bar. Cool, knowing smile. Pixel art.  
**History:** Talented jazz musician from the city who burned out from the scene and the pressures. Came to Highdew Valley on a whim for a "creative retreat" and found the perfect rhythm in the valley's pace and the herb's inspiration. Opened the lounge to create a space where people connect deeply – over music, stories, and good smoke (or alternatives). Knows everyone's story and offers sage (pun) advice.  
**Personality:** Extremely chill, witty, great listener and storyteller. Philosophical.  
**Likes:** Smooth balanced strains, live music, meaningful conversations, helping others unwind.  
**Dislikes:** Drama, uptight people, bad vibes.  
**Heart Events:** Private late-night music session where he plays a song inspired by you, help him with a lounge renovation or event, vulnerable talk about his past burnout and finding peace.

**5. Dr. Sage "Leaf" Rivera**  
**Role:** Herbalist and clinic owner. Medicinal focus.  
**Appearance:** Warm woman in her early 40s, shoulder-length dark hair with subtle green highlights, wearing a white lab coat over comfortable earth-tone clothes, stethoscope with a small leaf pendant, gentle caring expression, perhaps holding a mortar and pestle or topical jar. Pixel art.  
**History:** Former rising star pharmacologist at a big corporation. Became disillusioned after seeing how profit motives overrode patient well-being and suppressed natural remedies. Left everything behind after a personal health scare that conventional medicine couldn't fully address but cannabis did. Found the valley through research contacts and established the clinic to integrate evidence-based herbalism with modern care. Passionate about education and access.  
**Personality:** Compassionate, intelligent, calm under pressure. Scientific but open to traditional wisdom.  
**Likes:** Strains with documented therapeutic profiles (pain, anxiety, sleep, creativity), patient success stories, research data.  
**Dislikes:** Misinformation, over-commercialization without care, stigma.  
**Heart Events:** Assist in a clinical "trial" of a new strain blend, personal story about her journey, romantic or deep friendship moment involving healing and vulnerability.

**6. Zephyr "Mystic" Moon**  
**Role:** Alchemist at Mystic Spire. Advanced/rare supplies and lore.  
**Appearance:** Enigmatic person (can be interpreted any gender), flowing silver-white hair, wearing layered robes in deep greens, purples, and golds with celestial and leaf embroidery, mystical tattoos visible on hands/arms, holding a glowing crystal or ornate vial. Serene, knowing expression. Pixel art.  
**History:** Descended from (or claims to be) the valley's original indigenous stewards who first cultivated and revered the "spirit plants" for ceremony, healing, and connection. Lives reclusively in the old stone spire/tower on the edge of town, following biodynamic and lunar cycles for growing. Experiments with crystal elixirs, sound frequencies, and ancient techniques passed down. Helps the community with "special" preparations while guarding deeper mysteries of the valley.  
**Personality:** Poetic, enigmatic, profoundly wise and kind. Speaks in metaphors and riddles sometimes. Deeply connected to nature's rhythms.  
**Likes:** Landrace and heirloom strains, full moon harvests, crystals and minerals, respect for ancient ways.  
**Dislikes:** Disrespect for nature, rushed modern methods without soul, pollution of any kind.  
**Heart Events:** Participate in a moonlit ritual or biodynamic planting, uncover a valley secret together, profound conversation about interconnectedness and your place in the valley's story.

**7. Willow "Woody" Branch**  
**Role:** Carpenter and builder.  
**Appearance:** Sturdy, practical woman in her 30s-40s, short practical brown hair, flannel shirt rolled up, tool belt full of hammers/saws, jeans, warm no-nonsense smile, perhaps holding blueprints or a piece of wood. Pixel art.  
**History:** Born in Highdew Valley to a long line of builders and woodworkers. Family traditionally used local sustainable materials. When the cannabis economy boomed, she adapted the trade to specialize in high-efficiency greenhouses, drying barns, and cozy homes with hemp insulation and natural finishes. Proud of creating spaces that support the growers' lifestyle. Practical and grounded.  
**Personality:** Straight-talking, reliable, dry wit, values quality and sustainability.  
**Likes:** Efficient designs, reclaimed or local materials, strong work ethic, perhaps energizing sativas for focus.  
**Dislikes:** Waste, shortcuts in construction, flimsy work.  
**Heart Events:** Collaborate on designing and building a dream greenhouse or expansion for your farm, help with a community build project, personal talk about legacy and building things that last (literal and metaphorical).

**8. Penny "Penny" Copper**  
**Role:** General store owner. Daily needs and gossip.  
**Appearance:** Friendly middle-aged woman, short curly hair, wearing a practical apron over a blouse, glasses perched on nose, warm but shrewd expression, often behind counter with ledger or arranging goods. Pixel art.  
**History:** Ran the general store with her late husband for decades. Knows every resident's preferences, habits, and secrets (but only shares the harmless ones). The store has adapted to the valley's specialty economy while still stocking everyday items. She supports small growers fiercely and has seen many newcomers come and go – she hopes you'll stay and thrive.  
**Personality:** Chatty, helpful, motherly to the community, loves a good (kind) gossip session. Sharp business sense.  
**Likes:** Local products, repeat customers, community harmony, useful daily items.  
**Dislikes:** Rude customers, outsiders who don't respect local ways, waste.  
**Heart Events:** Help with a store inventory or special promotion, mediate or hear a town drama she needs advice on, warm friendship moments over tea and stories.

**9. Finn "Hook" Waters**  
**Role:** Fisherman and river expert.  
**Appearance:** Weathered older man (60s), kind wrinkled eyes, fishing hat with lures, vest with many pockets, simple shirt and waders or boots, holding a fishing rod or a nice catch. Calm, patient demeanor. Pixel art.  
**History:** His family has fished the Highdew River for generations, long before the cannabis boom. He adapted gracefully as the economy shifted, now supplying fresh fish to the community and sometimes guiding "herb tourists" or helping with riverbank restoration. Knows old stories of "spirit fish" that appeared near legendary grows and secret spots where the water is purest. Patient teacher.  
**Personality:** Quietly wise, patient, loves sharing stories and the peace of the river.  
**Likes:** Fresh catches, the rhythm of the river, respectful anglers, relaxing strains.  
**Dislikes:** Overfishing, littering the river, impatience.  
**Heart Events:** Teach you advanced fishing or reveal a legendary spot, share old valley fishing/grow legends, quiet reflective moments by the water.

**10. Grit "Rocky" Boulder**  
**Role:** Miner and explorer of Crystal Caverns.  
**Appearance:** Young man in his late 20s, rugged handsome, short messy dark hair, stubble, wearing practical mining clothes with helmet or headlamp, sturdy boots, determined but friendly expression, perhaps holding a pickaxe or glowing crystal sample. Pixel art.  
**History:** Grew up in Highdew Valley. His father was a miner who died in a tragic cave-in years ago while searching for richer mineral veins to support the growing community. Grit vowed to make the mines safe and productive again. He maps new tunnels, extracts valuable terpene-rich minerals and resin geodes used in premium fertilizers and concentrates. Carries some survivor's guilt but channels it into protecting others and honoring his dad's legacy. Brave and reliable underground.  
**Personality:** Tough exterior, loyal heart, a bit quiet or brooding at first but opens up. Protective of the valley.  
**Likes:** Adventure, reliable gear, strong recovery strains, stories of the deep earth.  
**Dislikes:** Recklessness underground, those who disrespect the mines or his father's memory.  
**Heart Events:** Accompany him on a deep mine expedition, help recover a family heirloom or memento from the caves, talk about loss, courage, and finding light in dark places.

**Role:** Artist and muralist.  
**Appearance:** Creative young woman in her mid-20s, paint-splattered overalls or clothes, colorful streaks in dark hair, holding a sketchbook and paints, artistic jewelry (leaf or crystal earrings), expressive, dreamy eyes. Pixel art.  
**History:** Visited Highdew Valley during a summer festival on a whim, drawn by rumors of inspiring light and creative energy. The combination of the natural beauty, the vibrant plant life, and the welcoming community sparked something profound. She stayed, converting an old shed into a studio. Her art – paintings, murals, and illustrations of strains, growers, and valley scenes – has become iconic locally and is starting to gain outside recognition. Finds deep inspiration in the plants and people.  
**Personality:** Passionate, emotional, empathetic, sees beauty everywhere. Can be a bit scattered or in her own world.  
**Likes:** Visually striking or uniquely colored buds, artistic collaborations, strains that spark creativity (cerebral sativas/hybrids).  
**Dislikes:** Blandness, lack of authenticity, destruction of natural or artistic beauty.  
**Heart Events:** Paint a mural together on your farm or in town, model for a portrait or help inspire a new series, deep talks about art as connection and healing.

**Appearance:** Warm, plump woman in her 40s, big genuine smile, flour-dusted apron over comfortable dress, short curly hair, rosy cheeks, often holding a tray of brownies or gummies or a mixing bowl. Nurturing aura. Pixel art.  
**History:** Long-time valley resident. After a close family member struggled with chronic pain and found relief through cannabis, she began experimenting with infused baking to make medicine more approachable and enjoyable. Her "special" brownies, cookies, and gummies became legendary for taste and consistency. She now runs a small kitchen supplying the lounge, clinic, and locals. Loves feeding people literally and figuratively.  
**Personality:** Motherly, generous, joyful, loves nurturing through food and care.  
**Likes:** High-quality buds for infusion, creative recipes, seeing people enjoy her treats and feel better.  
**Dislikes:** Waste of good ingredients, unkindness, rushed or low-quality work.  
**Heart Events:** Collaborate on a new signature edible recipe using your harvest, help with a big community feast or festival baking, warm conversations about family, comfort, and finding sweetness in life.
